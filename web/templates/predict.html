How predictions are calculated

Times are estimated using Riegel's formula: T₂ = T₁ × (D₂ / D₁)1.06

Where T₁ is your seed time over distance D₁, and T₂ is the predicted time for distance D₂. The exponent 1.06 reflects the well-documented fact that pace slows slightly as distance increases.

The formula assumes a maximal, even-paced effort similar to your seed activity. It does not account for course profile, weather, or heat acclimatisation. Results are most reliable when the target distance is within 2× of the seed (rows without a ~ prefix).

When your current form (TSB from the Fitness page) is negative, predicted times are adjusted slightly slower to reflect accumulated fatigue. When you are race-fresh (TSB ≥ +20), times are adjusted 3% faster.
