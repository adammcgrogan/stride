How to read this

Think of training like a bank account. Every workout deposits fitness but also charges fatigue. Your form on any given day is roughly how much fitness you have left after paying off the fatigue.

Fitness — How much training you have banked over the past six weeks. It rises slowly with consistent work and drops when you take time off. A steady upward trend means you are genuinely getting fitter.
Fatigue — How hard your body has been working in the past seven days. It spikes after a big week and recovers quickly with rest. When Fatigue is well above Fitness, you are digging a hole.
Form — Fitness minus Fatigue. This is your readiness to perform right now. Negative means you are carrying tiredness. Positive means you have freshened up. Taper before a race to push Form into the +10 to +25 window.
Form < −10Overreaching — You have been pushing too hard. Rest now or risk illness and injury.
−10 to 0Fatigued — Normal during a hard training block. Do not expect personal bests right now.
0 to +10Fresh — Good balance of fitness and recovery. A solid state for hard sessions.
+10 to +25Race-ready — Peak form. Schedule your A-race or time trial here.
> +25Detrained — You have been resting too long. Fitness is beginning to slip away.
